Description of Sepsis

Sepsis is a serious condition resulting from the presence of harmful microorganisms in the blood or tissues. It triggers a chain reaction throughout the body, leading to inflammation, organ dysfunction, and potentially death if left untreated. Common symptoms include fever, rapid heart rate, difficulty breathing, and altered mental status.

Safety Precautions

  • Practice good hygiene, including regular handwashing.
  • Seek prompt medical attention for any signs of infection.
  • Complete prescribed antibiotic treatments as directed by healthcare providers.
  • Stay up-to-date with vaccinations to prevent certain infections.
  • Follow safe food handling practices to reduce the risk of foodborne illnesses.
  • Keep wounds clean and covered until they are fully healed.
